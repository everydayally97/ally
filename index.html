Problem

Millions of adults over 40 struggle with:

  • Filling out forms, utility setups, and tech use
  • Language, vision, and hearing barriers
  • Isolation, lack of guidance or support
  • Rapidly changing technologies they can’t keep up with

Tech today is made for the fast, not the forgotten.

Solution

A hybrid assistant platform combining:

  • AI-powered help to explain tasks, translate, and guide step-by-step
  • On-demand human assistants for calls, paperwork, and everyday errands
  • Simplified guides that teach technology, services, and self-management

Think Uber meets ChatGPT for aging and underserved communities.

Product

  • Chat interface (text and voice) with AI helper
  • Task tutorials and tech explainers
  • Ability to book real human help
  • Tiered pricing with basic and premium plans
  • Community support features for ongoing engagement

Market

  • 90M+ adults aged 40+ in the U.S.
  • 39M U.S. adults have functional illiteracy
  • Over $70B spent annually on elder tech, assistants, and care services
  • Growing need for accessible tech due to digital transformation

Initial target: Adults 40–75, living alone or with limited support, in urban and suburban areas.

Business Model

  • Subscription model ($10–$49/month)
  • Pay-per-task pricing ($5–$25 per task)
  • B2B2C partnerships with service providers
  • Upsell family plans and local community packages

Traction

  • MVP in development
  • Beta waitlist live
  • Community partners identified: local banks, clinics, youth volunteers
  • Plan to launch in 1 test city within 3 months
